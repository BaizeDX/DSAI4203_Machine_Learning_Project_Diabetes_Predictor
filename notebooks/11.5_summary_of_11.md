### 11.5 Phase Summary: Interim Findings After Notebook 11

Notebook 11 marks a major methodological upgrade in the project. It replaces the earlier holdout-centered workflow with a more reliable cross-validation framework and therefore closes the first boosting-optimization phase on much stronger evaluation grounds.

This summary is intentionally **interim** rather than final. Later notebooks continue the search by examining validation design more closely and by reorganizing the overall project narrative around the most defensible methodological choices.

### What Notebook 11 Improved

**Validation method**
- Earlier phase: repeated use of holdout-style comparisons
- Notebook 11 phase: stratified cross-validation with Out-of-Fold evaluation

**Evaluation quality**
- Earlier phase: model scores were easier to compare than to fully trust
- Notebook 11 phase: performance estimates became more stable and more realistic

**Feature processing**
- Earlier phase: feature engineering experiments clarified what should be kept
- Notebook 11 phase: the stronger workflow preserves original features while integrating useful engineered features more carefully

**Generalization control**
- Earlier phase: model refinement focused mainly on local score improvement
- Notebook 11 phase: early stopping and fold-wise evaluation reduce the risk of reading too much into one split

### Interim Interpretation

The fold AUCs recorded for Notebook 11 are tightly grouped, with a mean AUC of **0.725852**, a standard deviation of **0.001294**, and an OOF AUC of **0.725845** in the retained final 7-fold run. These results suggest that the project had moved onto a much more trustworthy evaluation footing by this stage.

At the same time, Notebook 11 is not the end of the story. The later supporting notebooks revisit evaluation from two additional angles:

- whether tail-based validation may better reflect leaderboard behavior
- whether the cross-validation fold count itself should be adjusted for the final workflow

### Position in the Full Series

The best way to read Notebook 11.5 is as a **mid-experiment reflection**:

- Notebooks 01-10 identify the strongest model family and the main tuning direction.
- Notebook 11 strengthens the methodology behind those model choices.
- Notebooks 12-13 continue refining how model quality should be evaluated, eventually retaining 7-fold as the most practical balance between performance, stability, and runtime.
- Notebook 13.5 then provides the full project-level summary.
