### 10.5 Phase Review: Notebooks 01-10

This document summarizes the first major phase of the project, covering exploratory analysis, simple baselines, early model-family exploration, and the initial boosting-focused search.

### What This Phase Established

- The dataset is large, tabular, and moderately imbalanced, so evaluation design matters from the start.
- Simple baselines are useful for orientation, but they are not strong enough for the final project target.
- The KNN + MLP exploration was informative as a model-family check, but it did not become the preferred direction.
- Tree-based boosting quickly emerged as the strongest practical path for this dataset.

### Early Boosting Progression

- **Notebook 04: Default XGBoost baseline**  
  This notebook established the first strong boosting reference point and justified continued work on XGBoost.

- **Notebook 04.5: Default LightGBM baseline**  
  This comparison notebook provided an apples-to-apples baseline check against XGBoost under a closely aligned setup.

- **Notebooks 05-08: Iterative XGBoost refinement**  
  These notebooks tested imbalance handling, model capacity, feature engineering, and learning-rate / feature-selection adjustments. The sequence gradually improved the model, but it also revealed that score gains under a fixed holdout split can be difficult to trust.

- **Notebook 09: Ensemble checkpoint**  
  The ensemble test showed that combining strong candidates can be informative, even when it does not become the final preferred method.

- **Notebook 10: Comparative review**  
  This notebook closed the early experimentation phase by comparing the main holdout-based model variants in one place.

### Main Limitation of the Early Phase

The early notebooks improved model quality, but they still relied heavily on holdout-style evaluation. That makes this phase valuable for model discovery, yet weaker as a basis for final methodological claims.

The key lesson is not only that boosting works well, but also that later notebooks must strengthen the validation protocol before a final recommendation can be trusted.

### Transition to the Next Phase

The next step is therefore methodological rather than purely model-based:

- move from holdout-centered comparison toward more reliable cross-validation
- separate exploratory score improvements from trustworthy score improvements
- make the final project narrative depend on validation quality as well as model quality

Notebook 11 begins that transition.
